Carrot Buddy

Problem:

Currently, students across the nation are facing pressing challenges to their mental and physical wellbeing due to remote-learning and the lifestyle transition from physical to completely virtual. Our team aimed to improve student’s self-care and productivity levels by designing a solution that is engaging and interactive, providing an environment where individuals can manage their tasks, goals, and habits for a healthier and happier lifestyle.

Problem Statement:

How might we improve student’s self-care and productivity during remote-learning?

Research Objective:

Gain stronger insight to what issues our target users are facing being in quarantine through external sources with online journal reviews that have been measuring students’ performance metric levels since start of remote-learning.

User Research:

I conducted 8 interviews with university students (Drexel University, University of Pennsylvania, Temple University, and Penn State University) to determine the best pathway to a solution using the collection of data to drive my process. This includes contextual research of walkthrough of students’ daily remote routines, competitive market analysis, and survey data.

Storyboarding:

Created a character that students could relate to (empathize with) and devise a plot that users would more likely to remember. Stories motivate us to do certain things and can act as a persuasive communication tool. Revert back to the Psychology of Motivation and attempting to develop a feelng of connectedness.
